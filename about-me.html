About Kat

Kat Cattery is a renowned researcher and designer in the field of feline ergonomics and cat-human interactions. Her passion for creating harmonious co-spaces for cats and humans stems from a lifelong love for both animals and design. Growing up surrounded by a variety of pets, including several adored feline companions, Kat developed a deep understanding of the unique needs and behaviors of cats.

Kat's journey into the field of feline ergonomics began during her undergraduate studies in Industrial Design at Meow University. Inspired by her own cats' interactions with furniture and living spaces, she embarked on a mission to explore the intersection of design and feline well-being. Her studies delved into the ways in which ergonomic principles could be applied to cat furniture and environments, ultimately leading her to pursue a PhD in Ergonomic Design at the prestigious University of Purrington.

Throughout her academic and professional career, Kat's major inspirations have been the cats themselves. She believes that observing their natural behaviors, preferences, and comfort-seeking tendencies is key to creating truly effective and engaging designs. By studying the subtle nuances of feline body language, Kat has developed a keen eye for understanding how cats interact with their surroundings and how their well-being can be enhanced through thoughtful design interventions.

Beyond her work, Kat enjoys a whimsical and enchanting life outside the realm of academia. She spends her free time exploring magical forests, engaging in spirited conversations with woodland creatures, and embarking on imaginary adventures with her feline companions. Known for her whimsical fashion sense and collection of vintage cat-themed accessories, she brings a touch of playfulness and joy to everything she does.

Kat Cattery's tireless dedication to improving the lives of cats and humans through her research, innovative designs, and imaginative spirit has made her a beloved figure in the field. Her work continues to inspire designers, researchers, and cat lovers worldwide, as she strives to create a more comfortable and enriching world for our feline friends.

Research projects

In which I describe major research projects and their impact.

Enhancing Ergonomics in Cat-Human Spaces

Funding source: National Institute of Feline Health and Wellness (NIFHW)

In this groundbreaking research project, Kat Cattery focused on investigating ways to improve the ergonomic design of shared living and working spaces for cats and humans. The study aimed to enhance the physical and psychological well-being of both species by creating comfortable and functional environments. The project received generous funding from the NIFHW, an organization dedicated to advancing feline health and promoting harmonious cat-human interactions. The funding allowed for extensive data collection, innovative design experiments, and the development of ergonomic guidelines that benefited both cats and humans in cohabitation settings.

Exploring User Experience in Cat-Friendly Office Environments

Funding Source: Corporate Grant from MeowTech Corporation

Kat Cattery collaborated with MeowTech Corporation, a leading provider of cat-friendly office solutions, to investigate the user experience of employees working in cat-friendly office environments. The project aimed to understand how the presence of cats in the workplace impacted employee well-being, productivity, and job satisfaction. With the generous support of a corporate grant from MeowTech Corporation, Kat Cattery conducted surveys, interviews, and on-site observations in various office settings. The findings not only shed light on the benefits and challenges of cat-friendly workplaces but also influenced the design of future office spaces that seamlessly integrate cats into the work environment.

Designing Ergonomic Cat Furniture for Optimal Comfort

Funding Source: Kitty Comfort Foundation

The Kitty Comfort Foundation, a non-profit organization dedicated to improving the well-being of cats, provided funding for Kat Cattery's research project on designing ergonomic cat furniture. The study aimed to develop innovative furniture designs that prioritize feline comfort and promote healthy behaviors such as scratching, stretching, and resting. Through a combination of scientific research, user feedback, and iterative prototyping, Kat Cattery explored materials, shapes, and features that would enhance the overall well-being of cats. The support from the Kitty Comfort Foundation enabled extensive testing and the creation of practical and aesthetically pleasing cat furniture designs that have the potential to positively impact the lives of countless feline companions.
